• Hello there...

    Beware, this is an early demo:

    1. Click the deck button below to view its book
    2. Drag it onto the blank space to the left to get a stack of cards
    3. From there, you can drag cards around in the main area
    4. NOTE: the drag-and-drop UI is quite janky right now, and will change substantially; for now:
      • drag start is delayed until the mouse leaves the card, not on mouse down
      • relative-Y value matters both when starting a drag, and when dropping onto a stack of cards
      • Left click to turn a card over
      • Right click to flip a card into reversed orientation
        NOTE: firefox users may hold Shift for native context menu

    Coming Soon:

    1. Revamped card stack and drag/drop UX
    2. Shuffling: riffle 2 stacks, auto RNG shuffle, etc
    3. First class touch event support for tablets and phones
    4. Hands at least 2 of them for holding cards
    5. Defined places on the table: denote a given card or stack as a named place on the table
      • e.g. "this stack is the discard pile"
      • e.g. "this card represents the Past, this one the Present, this one the Future"
      • places will persist even after any cards there are removed, and will serve as a UX anchor for "maybe but card(s) here"
    6. Manipulation of held cards: fan them, turn them, etc
    7. More decks, and ability to import arbitrary image assets as new cards, decks, etc
    8. Deck editing
    9. Persistence of table state
